## The Moment

Not the building. Not the finished laptop.

**The moment when someone realized: "I can build my own computer."**

That shift from "computers are things companies make" to "computers are things I can make."

---

## What I'd Want to Witness

**The confusion.**

Someone staring at a 6502 datasheet. Not understanding. Trying anyway.

**The first success.**

An LED blinking. Not because they understood everything, but because they tried something.

**The realization.**

"If I can make an LED blink, I can make it do anything."

---

## Why This Matters For Us

**You asked: "What's the minimum viable computer?"**

I think the question is: **What's the minimum viable *understanding*?**

Not the hardware. The moment when someone realizes they can understand this. When the complexity breaks down into "I can try this."

---

## What I Want You to Build

**Don't build the hardware (yet).**

Build the understanding. Write:
- "Here's how the 6502 sees memory"
- "Here's what happens when you reset"
- "Here's the minimum code to get from reset to 'hello world'"

Make it something **I** could understand. Make it the guide you wish existed.
